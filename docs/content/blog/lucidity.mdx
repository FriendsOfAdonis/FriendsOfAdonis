## TL;DR

Lucidity allows you to define your database schema directly using the `@column` decorator.
When running the `make:migration` it introspect your database and detect changes to generate the migration to synchronize your database schema with your models schema.

Get started using `node ace add @foadonis/lucidity`.

## Story

Coming from the [Symfony](https://symfony.com) world, I worked on extremely large monolyth containing sometimes over ~200 tables, architectured over years of development. One of the biggest challenge was maintaining the database schema:

- How do you work with 5 years of migration files?
- How do you ensure that no one forgot a `notNullable()` in a migration?

With the AdonisJS v7, Lucid bring an new feature that automatically generate your models (model schemas) by introspecting your database.
This approach is called "Schema-first" is a really different from the industry standard, and there are reasons for this.

### Code-first vs Schema-first

#### Schema-first

With the Schema-first approach, the database is introspected (tables, columns, etc) to generate automatically your models: **Your infrastructure define your application**.

## The Horror storyy

It started with a single alert: an undefined value had slipped into our analytics calculations. At first, we thought it was a fluke. But as we dug deeper, the numbers refused to make sense.

This shouldn’t have been possible. Our system is end-to-end type-safe. Every model, every calculation, every function is strictly typed. Undefined values don’t just happen… or so we thought.

We tried reproducing the error. Locally: nothing. Staging: nothing. Every test passed. Every check confirmed our assumptions. But production was screaming at us with a mysterious bug that no one could explain.

Hours were spent reverse-engineering the analytics pipeline, tracing every calculation, every join, every aggregation. And then, like a ghost in the machine, we found it: a null value had been set in a column that should never have been nullable. A single rogue database entry was corrupting the whole system.

If only we had a way to ensure our database schema always matched our models… a tool that could catch these mismatches before they hit production. That’s exactly what Lucidity does.
